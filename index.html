Field guide

What are indoor positioning, indoor localization, and indoor navigation?

These terms are closely related but not identical. The distinction helps readers understand how measurements, estimated locations, and movement guidance become useful services inside buildings where satellite positioning is weak or unavailable.

📍

Indoor positioning

Indoor positioning estimates the position of a person, smartphone, robot, asset, or sensor inside buildings using radio signals, inertial sensors, maps, vision, magnetic fields, barometers, or combinations of these measurements.

🏢

Indoor localization

Indoor localization determines where the user or object is in an indoor reference frame, such as a coordinate, floor, room, corridor, zone, landmark, or building-level map representation.

🧭

Indoor navigation

Indoor navigation uses position, heading, motion state, route information, and spatial context to guide movement, support wayfinding, enable location-aware services, and connect positioning outputs to decisions.
